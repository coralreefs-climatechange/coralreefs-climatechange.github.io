Ocean Acidification

Why does it happen?

Since the industrial revolution, anthropogenic Carbon Dioxide emissions have increased exponentially. We burn fossil fuels for transportation, industry, electricity, agriculture and more, and these processes pollute the air with CO2. The ocean absorbs a great majority of those emissions, causing problems for life underwater. As water acidity increases, more problems arise.

As CO2 accumulates, a series of chemical reactions take place that reduce the ocean’s pH level and lower the concentrations of carbonate ions and calcium carbonate minerals. This lack of carbonate makes it difficult for calcifying organisms like corals to grow and rebuild themselves. The heightened acidity further breaks down their skeletons and they can easily dissolve.

Global temperatures and Carbon Dioxide emissions are at unprecedented levels. Scroll down to see how rising temperatures affect corals.
